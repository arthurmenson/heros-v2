Frequently Asked Questions

What is chronic care management?

Chronic care management (CCM) is comprehensive, ongoing healthcare for patients with chronic conditions like diabetes, heart disease, or COPD. It includes medication management, care coordination, health monitoring, and personalized treatment planning to help you manage your conditions and prevent complications.

Who is eligible for CCM services?

You're eligible if you have two or more chronic conditions lasting 12+ months, take multiple medications, or need ongoing medical management. Common qualifying conditions include diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, COPD, arthritis, chronic kidney disease, and depression.

Is CCM covered by insurance?

Yes! Medicare and many commercial insurance plans cover chronic care management services. We handle all insurance verification and billing. Even if not fully covered, CCM often saves money by preventing expensive ER visits and hospitalizations.

How does CCM work with my current doctor?

CCM enhances your existing care rather than replacing it. We coordinate with your primary care doctor and specialists, sharing progress reports and care plans. Your doctors remain the primary decision-makers for your treatment while we provide ongoing support and monitoring between visits.

What's included in my care plan?

Your personalized care plan includes medication management, health goal tracking, symptom monitoring, lifestyle recommendations, emergency action plans, and coordination schedules with your healthcare providers. Plans are updated monthly based on your progress and changing needs.

Can I cancel my CCM service?

Yes, you can cancel anytime with 30 days notice. We offer a 30-day money-back guarantee if you're not satisfied with the service. However, most patients see significant improvements in their health management within the first 3 months.
